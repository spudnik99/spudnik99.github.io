Introduction

Location, location, location...this storymap is a basic briefer that highlights geographic features that impact Mexican society.

Putting it on the map

Mexico is located in the Western Hemisphere, bordering the USA on the north, Guatemala and Belize to the South.

Is it a tropical paradise?

The Tropic of Cancer bissects the country horizontally, but most of the land mass is not in the tropics.

The left coast

A long coastline with some natural harbors has allowed Mexico to trade with China beginning with the Ming Dynasty in 1573.

The heart of Mexico

When most non-Mexicans think of Mexico, mariachis, tequila, and tacos come to mind. All them have their origin in the neo-volcanic transerval range.

Green area on map is the neo-volcanic range.

Where most Mexicans live

The Mexican populace lives mostly in a relatively small physiographic region, the pine-oak forests known as the neo-volcanic transerval range. All them have their origin in the neo-volcanic transerval range.

Blue towers on map are localities, height is total population.

Living with volcanoes

The neo-volcanic transerval range is dotted with thousands of volcanoes of varying sizes. Most of this region is a high plain, the abrupt change in elevation of volcanic structures created "islands" of isolated biodiversity, Mexico has the largest variety of pine trees in the world.

Red dots are volcanoes.
